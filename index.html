    Exotic dance moves

    Yes, you are right as part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To
    answer your questions, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

    Polynessian chicken dance
    A little known but very influencial dating as far as 300BC, a whole village
    would dance around like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".
    Icelandic brownian shuffle
    Before the Icelanders developed fire as means of getting warm, they use to practice
    this dance, which involved huddling close in a circle on the floor, and shuffling
    their bodies around in imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movement. One of my fellow
    student used to say that he thought these dance inspired modern styles such twerking.
    Arctic robot dance
    An interesting of historic missing information, english explores in the 1960s
    believed to have discussed the new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted
    movements, beind practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada.Later on
    however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
